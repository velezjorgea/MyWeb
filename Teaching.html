Teaching Philosophy

Teaching is not only about delivering knowledge to students, but also about helping them construct knowledge for themselves. Student-centred knowledge construction requires four steps: 1) asking questions, 2) finding and vetting sources to acquire data and information, and 3) deploying strong analytical skills, 4) skills for working and co-operating within the group. Whenever I teach, I focus on developing these four competencies in my students.

As far as my evaluation methods are concerned, I am always trying to deviate from the normal evaluation approaches. I usually use formative assessment as students are learning. In every subject I often adjust the teaching based on what students know at each point. I use means of technology to carry out my evaluations Live Quiz Games on "Quizlet", and instant feedback on "Socrative". For written exams, using the student's performance I design my questionnaires, laying down questions that push the student to develop analytical and critical answers. For instance, in Business Economics I use real case scenarios to set up the problem and ask the students to analyse it See an example.
