Papers (Journal Articles in English)

  • Job Market Paper: Innovation Subsidies and Business Cycle (Sep, 2018). Jointly with Busom, I. Paper Under review: Review of Economics and Statistics

    Summary We find that the allocation of subsidies did not change significantly during the crisis. We also find that the multiplier of public support (also called the degree of additionality) is higher when firms participate twice in public programs than when they participate once. Also, the estimated multiplier is found to be higher during recession years when firms participate twice. Finally, the effect is strong and more persistent regarding the allocation of human capital rather than the spending intensity. These results suggest that public support to firm’s R\&D effort is more effective during recessions for firms that have sustained innovation activities and are likely to participate for more than one year. So that, the main highlight is that timing and length of participation matter. Access

  • Innovation, public support and Productivity in Colombia: A cross-industry comparison. Jointly with Busom, I. World Development (November, 2017).

    Summary We contribute new evidence on the relationship between public support, innovation and productivity at the firm level by investigating several under-explored issues. Using firm-level data for Colombian Companies, we try to identify and compare the profile of firms that have access to public support for innovation in manufacturing and service industries separately. In the second block of research, we examine whether the association between the introduction of innovations and productivity varies across the productivity distribution; third, we distinguish between technological and non-technological innovation, since the latter may be especially relevant in the service industries relative to manufacturing.

    This research has been published in a top-ranked journal- World Development-2017, It also received a prize as the best paper presented in the innovation field at the 4th PhD-Student workshop in Industrial and Public Economics (WIPE). This paper was also presented at the Interamerican Development Bank (IDB) as part of the first conference on Economics of Innovation in Latin-America held in June, 2017. Access
